EC2 Storage section

1.) EBS Volume:
It is a network drive you attach to your instances while they run
It allows your instance to persist data, even afte their termination
They can be mounted at one instance at a time
They are bound to a specific zone
They might be a lit of network, there might be latency
They can be detached between different ec2 instances
An EBS Volume of one zone cannot be attched to another zone
It needs to have a provisoned capacity 
(size in DBS and iops)
User gets billed for provisioned capacity

EBS - Delete on Termination attribute
They EBS Volume can deleted on terminiation 
This behaviour can be controlled by AWS CPNSOLE/CLI
Use Case:Preserve root volume when instance is termintaed

EBS Multi-attach
These are instances where multiple ebs volumes are attached to EC2 Instance
